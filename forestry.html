Concessions

The sustainability of a timber Company depends largely on sustainable forest resources. We therefore attach a lot of seriousness to the conservation of our forest reserves/concessions. The success of this is founded on the principle that only collaborative action will result in significant change. By this, we work closely with local communities, FSD, regional, national and international conservation groups and companies to ensure that our practices are sustainable. This is evidenced by our forest certification programme which ensures that all our forest operations meet Sustainable Forest Management Standards. i.e. Environmentally Responsible Socially Beneficial and Economically Viable.  We always make sure that, our customers and other consumers of our products are buying from a source that complies with sustainable forests management practices and thereby providing an incentive for responsible forest management.

Re-afforestaion

In order to resuscitate some of the already degraded forests and for the company to be self-reliant and consistent in the timber business, vigorous reforestation projects are being embarked upon. It is the view of the company that planted forests can equally provide an array of social, economic and environmental benefits, and can contribute to satisfying the world’s needs for forest products. This initiative is intended to complement and reduce pressure and promote the restoration and conservation of natural forests. Within this framework, the company has already reforested about 100 ha of the Barekese Dam catchment area along the Offin River and about 171.3 ha at the Bia Tawya afforestation project. Again, an area of over 1,200ha of the Tain II degraded Forest Reserve at Nsoatre under the Sunyani Forest District has over the years been subjected to a progressive establishment of an Industrial Teak Plantation project and from 2016 added the planting of Mahogany tree species. So far, a total area of 400ha has been planted; comprising 350ha of Teak and still planting.
